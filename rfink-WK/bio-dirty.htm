Richard Fink - Biographical Information

Richard Fink is a consulting font technologist and web developer focused on web fonts for multiple languages that look good on any screen and any device. His technical skills with fonts evolved out of his interest in screen readability and the revolution in human expression summed up in what may have been the first use of a true scalable web font in a blog logo and tag line to ever appear:

Mentored and encouraged by Microsoft's screen font and typography innovator, the late Bill Hill, and also Adobe Type's Thomas Phinney, Rich began blogging about web fonts and screen readability issues in 2009 at Readable Web, quickly earning a mention in web standards evangelist Jeffrey Zeldman's ground-breaking book Designing With Web Standards for his expert analysis of Microsoft's proprietary Embedded Open Type (EOT) web font format.

Rich was a featured speaker FontConf in St. Paul, Minnesota, along with other web typography visionaries like Dave Crossland who would soon become the driving force behind Google Web Fonts. Rich was also a featured speaker at the annual ATYPI conference held in Dublin, Ireland. Rich is a frequent contributor to Typedrawers, and the Google Web Fonts Forum.

Rich has also written full-length articles for the long-running web design publication AListApart. First, with the seminal Web Fonts At The Crossing - parts of which made their way into then author and now Senior Designer at Booking.com, Zoe Mickley Gillenwater's book Stunning CSS3 and next, with an article titled The Look That Says Book – an analysis of techniques for acheiving Hyphenation & Justification in web typography..

Rich also helped push the early adoption of web fonts in a number of ways. First, with his development of EOTFAST, a tool for producing true compressed Embedded OpenType fonts in a way that avoided copyright or patent infringement of Monotype's MicroType compression algorithm – a barrier that most in the industry at the time wrongly assumed was insurmountable. Today, there are other EOT conversion tools, of course, but even so, six years later, the documentation that accompanied EOTFAST, explaining the requirements for successful TTF-to-EOT conversion, is still being referenced by type professionals today - 6 years and still counting after it's creation.

Second, Rich collaborated with Google's Paul Irish and Ethan Dunham of Fontspring to develop the backward-compatible “#iefix” @font-face syntax that‘s still used in hundreds of millions of web pages viewed by people every day. As documented in Eric Meyer's definitive reference CSS Fonts and CSS: The Definitive Guide.

Then, in collaboration with designer Garrick Van Buren, Rich was Font Director of Kernest Konstellations, a project that pioneered the extensive use of open source web fonts within working HTML design templates.

More recently, Rich has been involved with quality control efforts at Google Web Fonts with a focus on best practices, the development of advanced HTML web font test pages, and a new taxonomy for font character sets.

In keeping with the general move away from dedicated blogging sites, Rich has begun aggregating and commenting on Medium and Facebook under the name FontFriday. Follow FontFriday on Twitter at @FontFridayTweet.
