Richard Fink - Biographical Information

Richard Fink is a consulting font technologist and web developer focused on web fonts for multiple languages that look good on any screen and any device. His technical skills with fonts evolved out of his interest in screen readability and the revolution in human expression summed up in the tag line of his original blog Readable Web, in what may have been the first use of a true scalable web font in a blog logo and tag line ever to appear:

Mentored and encouraged by Microsoft's screen font and typography innovator, the late Bill Hill, and Adobe Type's Thomas Phinney, Rich began blogging about web fonts and screen readability in 2009, where his expert analysis of Microsoft's proprietary Embedded Open Type (EOT) web font format quickly earned a mention in web standards evangelist Jeffrey Zeldman's iconic book Designing With Web Standards.

Rich was a featured speaker at FontConf in St. Paul, Minnesota, along with other web typography visionaries like Dave Crossland, who would soon become the driving force behind Google Web Fonts. Rich was also a featured speaker at the annual ATYPI conference held in Dublin, Ireland. He's a frequent contributor to both Typedrawers, and the Google Web Fonts Forum.

Rich has also written full-length articles for the long-running web design publication AListApart. First came the seminal Web Fonts At The Crossing - key parts of which made their way into a book by the brilliant Zoe Mickley Gillenwater, now Senior Designer at Booking.com, titled Stunning CSS3. This was soon followed by The Look That Says Book – an article about the various techniques for achieving Hyphenation & Justification in web typography..

Rich also helped push the early adoption of web fonts in a number of ways. First, with his development of EOTFAST, a tool for producing true compressed Embedded OpenType fonts in a way that avoided copyright or patent infringement of Monotype's MicroType compression algorithm – a barrier that most in the industry at the time wrongly assumed was insurmountable. Today, there are other EOT conversion tools, of course, but even so, the comprehensive documentation that accompanied EOTFAST - carefully researched documentation explaining the technical ins and outs of preparing a TrueType font for successful TTF-to-EOT conversion, is still being referenced by type professionals today - 6 years and counting after it was published!

Next, Rich collaborated with Google's Paul Irish and Ethan Dunham of Fontspring to develop the backward-compatible “#iefix” @font-face syntax that‘s still used in hundreds of millions of web pages viewed by people every day. As documented in Eric A Meyer's references CSS Fonts and CSS: The Definitive Guide.

Then, in collaboration with designer Garrick Van Buren, Rich was Font Director of Kernest Konstellations, a project that pioneered the extensive use of open-source web fonts within working HTML design templates.

More recently, Rich has been involved with quality control efforts at Google Web Fonts with a focus on best practices for font construction, the development of advanced multilingual HTML web font test pages, and a new taxonomy for font character sets.

In keeping with the general move away from dedicated blogging sites, Readable Web remains static and instead, Rich aggregates links and posts comments at his managed Facebook Page FontFriday He also writes an occasional analysis or essay as FontFriday on Medium.com.
